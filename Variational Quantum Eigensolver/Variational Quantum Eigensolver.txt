Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) Explorer 
This is the most practical quantum algorithm for near-term devices. Here's what it covers:

The Core Problem: Find ground state energy E₀ and wavefunction |ψ₀⟩ for a Hamiltonian H
•	Classical: Diagonalize H (exponentially hard for large systems)
•	Quantum: Use quantum computer + classical optimizer
Why VQE Matters:
•	Works TODAY on NISQ (noisy) devices (no error correction needed)
•	Hybrid approach: Plays to strengths of both classical and quantum
•	Practical applications: Drug discovery, materials science, quantum chemistry
•	Industry adoption: Merck, Roche, IBM, Google already using it
6 Core Experiments:
1.	Hamiltonian & Ground State — Understanding quantum systems
2.	Pauli Measurements — How to measure ⟨Z⟩, ⟨X⟩, ⟨Y⟩ on quantum computer
3.	Energy Calculation — Compute total energy from Pauli terms
4.	Simple 1-Qubit VQE — Full optimization loop (E(θ) = cos(θ), find minimum)
5.	2-Qubit VQE — More realistic: H = 0.5·I - 0.5·Z₀ - 0.3·Z₁ + 0.2·Z₀Z₁
6.	Ansatz Design — Different circuit templates (single layer, hardware efficient, deep, problem-specific)
The Algorithm:
1. Define Hamiltonian H = Σᵢ hᵢ Pᵢ (as Pauli terms)
2. Choose parameterized ansatz |ψ(θ)⟩ = U(θ)|0⟩
3. Loop:
   a) Measure each Pauli term ⟨Pᵢ⟩
   b) Compute E(θ) = Σᵢ hᵢ⟨Pᵢ⟩
   c) Classical optimizer updates θ
   d) Until convergence
4. Return E₀ (ground state energy) and |ψ₀⟩
Key Advantages over Classical:
•	Linear (not exponential) scaling with qubits
•	Works on noisy devices
•	Can handle large Hilbert spaces
•	Hybrid efficiency: quantum for hard part, classical for optimization
